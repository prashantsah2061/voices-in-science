An archive in progress

Women in science from the Global South, restored to the record.

For generations, scientific history has centered women from the United States and Western Europe. The lives, discoveries, and obstacles of women scientists from Africa, Asia, Latin America, and the Middle East have been overlooked, minimized, or written out entirely. This archive begins the work of restoring them.

About this project

This project began as a final paper for HP 316, a Women's and Gender Studies course. Inspired by Audre Lorde's call to transform silence into language, by Jean Kilbourne's writing on the erasure of women from America's story, and by Rebecca Solnit's City of Women map project, it asks a simple question: who has scientific history been silent about, and how do we get them back?

Existing recovery projects do real work, but they tend to center women from the United States and Europe. The result is a partial record. Women like Tebello Nyokong, Quarraisha Abdool Karim, Janaki Ammal, and Wangari Maathai — whose research has shaped global health, agriculture, and the environment — remain largely absent from popular accounts of the women who built modern science.

The archive is small and growing. It depends on nominations from readers, students, teachers, and researchers around the world. If you know a woman scientist from the Global South whose story belongs here, please submit a nomination.
